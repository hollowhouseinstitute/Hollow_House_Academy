Overview
Hollow House Academy is a governance-first educational framework designed to support children, families, organizations, and communities through ethical, joyful, and developmentally respectful learning.
The framework treats education not as a sequence of lessons but as behavioral infrastructure developing across time.
Learning environments are designed to be:
Human-led — adults maintain final authority and responsibility
Governed — systems operate under explicit rules and constraints
Auditable — drift and misalignment remain visible
Relational — learning emerges through connection and environment
Fractal — development unfolds in nested cycles rather than linear tracks
This repository documents the structural foundations required to support that model.
Hollow House Academy is not a curriculum library.
It is an operating framework for ethical learning systems.

Design Principles
Human Authority
Education must preserve human judgment, responsibility, and ethical closure.
Technology may assist but must never replace accountable adults.
Fractal Learning
Children develop through cycles of curiosity, exploration, reflection, and integration.
Learning structures must respect these developmental rhythms.
Governed AI
AI tools can assist educators and learners but must operate inside visible and enforceable boundaries.
Relational Safety
Learning environments must protect emotional safety, autonomy, and dignity.
Transparency
Systems used in educational environments must remain inspectable and auditable.
Why This Exists
Modern educational systems often face structural problems:
technology deployed without governance
content systems that ignore developmental realities
learning environments optimized for efficiency rather than human growth
Hollow House Academy addresses these challenges by introducing governance as a foundational layer beneath learning systems.
Rather than regulating outcomes after problems occur, governance is integrated directly into the architecture of education.
